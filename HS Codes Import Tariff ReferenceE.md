# Scarf Export Essentials: HS Codes & Import Tariff Reference

An open reference for importers, sourcing teams, and compliance researchers covering scarf HS classification logic, material-based code selection, and indicative duty treatment across major markets.

**Updated:** April 2026  
**Coverage:** US HTS · EU TARIC · UK GTT · Canada · Australia · Japan

---

## Why the Classification Logic Matters

For customs purposes, a scarf is not simply a scarf. The applicable HS heading — and therefore the duty rate, quota implications, and regulatory obligations at destination — depends mainly on two variables:

- **Construction method**: woven vs. knit
- **Primary fiber content by weight**

Misclassification can result in underpayment of duties, overpayment, delayed clearance, or audit flags. For cashmere and other fine animal hair products, major markets may require that fiber content claims on labels match laboratory-verified composition.

> **Key principle:** Customs authorities classify goods by their objective characteristics at the time of importation — not by brand name, hang tag, or invoice description.

---

## Classification Decision Tree

### Step 1 — Is it knitted or crocheted?

If formed by interlocking loops (knit, crochet, warp-knit), classification generally starts under:

- **Chapter 61 → Heading 6117**

If not, continue below.

### Step 2 — Is it a handkerchief?

If the item is square and **no side exceeds 60 cm**, it may fall under:

- **Heading 6213**

Otherwise, scarves usually fall under:

- **Heading 6214**

### Step 3 — What is the predominant fiber by weight?

Common order of fiber-based subheading logic:

- Silk
- Wool / fine animal hair
- Synthetic fibers
- Artificial fibers
- Cotton
- Other textile materials

### Step 4 — Confirm country of origin

Duty treatment depends on **where the product was manufactured**, not simply where it was shipped from.

## HS Code Reference by Material

At the 6-digit level, HS codes are harmonized internationally. Individual countries may extend these codes for their own tariff schedules.

| HS Code | Description | Type | US Base Rate | EU Base Rate | Notes |
|---|---|---|---:|---:|---|
| 6214.10 | Scarves — silk or silk waste | Woven | 1.2% | 9% | Lowest US rate; verify silk purity |
| 6214.20 | Scarves — wool or fine animal hair (including cashmere) | Woven | 7% | 12% | Common classification for cashmere products |
| 6214.30 | Scarves — synthetic fibers | Woven | 11.3% | 12% | Polyester, acrylic, nylon as primary fiber |
| 6214.40 | Scarves — artificial fibers | Woven | 11.3% | 12% | Viscose, modal, lyocell/Tencel |
| 6214.90 | Scarves — other textile materials | Woven | 11.3% | 12% | Cotton, linen, and miscellaneous blends |
| 6117.10 | Shawls, scarves — knitted or crocheted | Knit | 7–17% | 12% | Rate varies by fiber sub-classification |

> **Common misclassification risk:** Infinity scarves and cowl-neck accessories may sometimes be reclassified under 6117 or, in some cases, under headgear-related classifications. A binding ruling may be advisable before first shipment.

## Key Classification Factors

### Construction

- Knit / crochet products usually fall under **Chapter 61**
- Woven products usually fall under **Chapter 62**

### Fiber dominance

Subheading is generally determined by the fiber of greatest weight.

Example:

- 55% wool / 45% cashmere → generally classified under wool / fine animal hair heading

### Dimensions

Square items with no side exceeding 60 cm may fall under the handkerchief heading instead of scarf heading.

### Ornamentation

Fringe and embroidery usually do not change the primary classification unless they dominate the product’s essential character.

## Import Duty Rates by Destination Market

Rates below are **MFN base rates** and may not reflect additional tariff overlays, trade remedies, or preferential origin treatment.

### United States

- Silk (6214.10): **1.2%**
- Wool / Cashmere (6214.20): **7%**
- Synthetic (6214.30): **11.3%**
- Cotton / Other (6214.90): **11.3%**

Notes:

- China-origin goods may face additional trade measures depending on current policy
- Origin review is often as important as classification review

### European Union

- Silk (6214.10): **9%**
- Wool / Cashmere (6214.20): **12%**
- Synthetic (6214.30): **12%**
- Cotton / Other (6214.90): **12%**

Notes:

- VAT applies at import
- Textile labeling, REACH, and sustainability rules may also apply

### United Kingdom

- Silk (6214.10): **9%**
- Wool / Cashmere (6214.20): **12%**
- Synthetic (6214.30): **12%**
- Cotton / Other (6214.90): **12%**

Notes:

- VAT applies at import
- Preferential origin treatment depends on applicable agreements

### Canada

- Silk (6214.10): **Free**
- Wool / Cashmere (6214.20): **14%**
- Synthetic (6214.30): **18%**
- Cotton / Other (6214.90): **18%**

Notes:

- GST and provincial taxes may apply
- Preferential tariff programs may reduce effective duty depending on origin

### Australia

- All scarves (6214.xx): **0%**

Notes:

- GST applies at import
- Preferential treatment depends on origin and documentation

### Japan

- Silk (6214.10): **3.2%**
- Wool / Cashmere (6214.20): **9.1%**
- Synthetic / Cotton (6214.30 / 6214.90): **9.1%**

Notes:

- Consumption tax applies
- Japanese-language labeling may be required

### Fiber content claims

Regulators in major markets have increased scrutiny of fiber labeling and cashmere composition claims, especially where premium animal fibers are involved.

### Origin sensitivity

Country of origin can significantly alter duty treatment and compliance obligations, even where the HS heading remains unchanged.

## China-Origin Shipments to the US: Why Extra Review Is Often Needed

### Base MFN rate

Base duty may range from:

- **7%** for wool / fine animal hair scarf classifications
- **11.3%** for many synthetic or other textile headings

### Additional tariff overlays

US import treatment for China-origin textile goods may include Section 301 measures or other overlays depending on current policy status.

### Customs entry treatment

Low-value shipment handling rules have changed in recent years, so importers should confirm whether formal entry and additional documentation are required.

### Origin verification

Where goods are claimed under a different origin strategy, supporting documentation should be reviewed carefully.

> **Practical takeaway:** For US-bound imports, classification review should be paired with origin review. The duty impact often depends not only on what the scarf is, but also on where customs determines it was made.

## Documentation Requirements at Entry

Typical documentation may include:

- Commercial invoice
- Packing list
- Certificate of Origin
- Fiber content test report
- Care and labeling compliance records

### Commercial invoice

Should typically include:

- HS code
- Fiber composition by weight
- Country of origin
- Unit value
- Product description

### Packing list

Usually includes:

- Quantity
- Gross / net weight
- Carton counts
- Carton dimensions

### Certificate of Origin

May be required for:

- FTA claims
- Preferential tariffs
- Origin verification

### Fiber content test report

For premium fibers such as cashmere, independent lab verification may be requested or commercially advisable.

### Care and labeling compliance

Destination-market labeling rules may require:

- Fiber content
- Country of origin
- Care instructions
- Local-language presentation in some markets

## Three Common HS Code Mistakes

### 1. Using the wrong heading for knit products

A knitted cashmere scarf incorrectly classified under woven heading 6214 may actually belong under 6117.

### 2. Declaring fiber content by marketing description rather than weight

A product marketed as a “cashmere blend” should still be classified according to the predominant fiber by weight.

Related reading:  
[The Great Cashmere War: Who Is Fooling You?](https://weaveessence.blogspot.com/2026/04/the-great-cashmere-war-who-is-fooling.html)

### 3. Ignoring dimension rules for square products

Square textile items with no side exceeding 60 cm may fall under the handkerchief heading 6213 rather than scarf heading 6214.
